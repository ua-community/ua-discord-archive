Search: All the major browser vendors have their own cultures, companies, codebases , communities, controversies, cults and probably many other things beginning with c. Yet *none* really went down this road. The economics are a compelling reason to invest in the opposite: Back then, search was the *only* profit center - and for the *browser* product of even the behemoths, that likely remains entirely true. Maximizing the value of a local  "second brain" would likely only result in *fewer* searches. Fewer searches *directly* results in less revenue. I've not been party to anyone intentionally defunding browser features for this reason, but it's a structural challenge to innovation (A company is... what earns revenue?). So search kept getting better and better, and browsers become clients for accessing search services - an unsurprising outcome of a structural incentive.
