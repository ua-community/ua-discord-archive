[ownership](https://www.inkandswitch.com/essay/local-first/#7-you-retain-ultimate-ownership-and-control) and [offline support](https://www.inkandswitch.com/essay/local-first/#3-the-network-is-optional) are different principles in local-first. ownership implies you can modify something offline, but just because you can modify it offline doesn't mean you own it. 

At the last point in this slide points out, one litmus test for ownership is if the app you're using goes out of business/stops working can you still do meaningful things with your data? Are they able to revoke access to your data without your consent?
