Was thinking about a few things that came out of (user) pushing on this over the last months, and some observations:

- While all the projects have some kind of "extensibility", they're VERY different from each other
- That's often because they're serving different use-cases, and those use-cases are not super clear
- Sometimes there's use-case overlap at a high level, but rarely if you go a layer or two down, or all the way to the metal
- Thus, the extensibility vector is implemented at wildly different layers, even accounting for architectural differences
- While folks kinda wanted *some* extensibility compat with today's browsers, *most* extensibility was providing entirely different capabilities
- No really unique devtools yet, which is interesting
- To me, EUP still feels pretty far away if you're not adding an LLM into the mix to "make" stuff in response to user inputs
- Seems all the projects had a reticence to just jam an LLM in there and call it a day - most of the extensibility work is a layer below that (at least)
