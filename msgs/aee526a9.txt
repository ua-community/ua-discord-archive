Interesting, but the conclusion seems completely wrong to me

> As we’ve seen, modern computers can only represent floating point numbers with a finite number of bits, and so numerical error is unavoidable. 
This is technically true, but the core issue isn't the numerical error, it's that this error happens at unknown times with no rules.

Everyone knows this, I feel like every developer has been taught that we usually use IEEE754 floats, but if dealing with money or similar things, use a fixed-precision data type and take care of rounding (= errors) yourself.

It's ridiculous to me that we are pretending the computer has a will of its own and we just have to alleviate issues that "just happen". We've made this problem, we knew about this problem, and we know how to fix it.
