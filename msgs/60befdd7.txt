Ultimately ATProto has certain trade-offs that centralize some things to support the decentralization of others. So, I'm sympathetic that you must work within these trade-offs when suggesting something that is specifically intended to be a part of ATProto. ATProto has centralization on the discovery layer (e.g., relays, firehoses and app view) so that it can decentralize data and (although this isn't fully realized yet) identity.

You admitted to me earlier that your design vests trust in the PDS host (the service provider that runs the PDS). This points to an uncomfortable detail of the design: the data no longer speaks for itself; it depends on the state of the PDS, and that may be subject to the whims of the PDS host.

It's not satisfying to say that the user should be discerning of their host. History informs us that users at scale do not have agency to be so discerning.
