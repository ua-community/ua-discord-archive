This led me to check their imprint and they are in fact a GbR - which is a  way on German law to do something together without forming a new legal entity.
Notably there is simply a contract between the people doing the thing, usually with some goal set. That goal doesn't HAVE to be making tons of money.
There is no way to invest in such a company, and there are no legal protections offered to the contributors.
Meaning, this is one of the (weaker) ways to form an entity that isn't inherently made for making money.
So this is in principle a good thing in my opinion. There is no real incentive to enshitify the product, sell data, etc, beyond the basic "I need money to live" or the participants.
It's very uncommon to see, as it also means there is no way to hire people, there are tax downsides, etc. but it can be a good way to build up to a coop (as founding that costs money).
I am not a lawyer of course
