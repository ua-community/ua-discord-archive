> A while ago, I had the idea for an address book abstraction that would allow DIDs to change around a PDS without impacting the integrity of the data inside it. It occurs to me that the same idea extended to AT URIs as a whole could save a lot of headache, if you do move forward on changing the AT URI syntax. This could be a good idea anyway, because you might want your PDS to internally redirect a link you originally posted 10 years ago if the website restructured since then. In my opinion, assuming the immutability of identity, and indeed references in general, was always one of ATProto's subtle foot guns, this predicament brings that to the fore.
