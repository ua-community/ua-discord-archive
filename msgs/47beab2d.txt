Once enough software is open source, it is inevitable that someone has made something that meets your need at some level (even if hoops and risks to get running). Now put that on a loop - over the last few decades software is both increasingly open source - and *expected to be free*... even by companies making billions. In fact, they depend on that software (labor) to be free. So while markets differ (economic/class differential) and some will last longer than others, the endgame of open source becoming popular/default for devs is that there's nobody left to pay the developers of it. In an economic environment that's 1) increasingly digital and 2) reliant an extracting this digital free labor to make quarterly number go up, open source accelerates this negative feedback loop, rapidly destroying civilization as we know it, leaving a software-based world in the hands of the few feudal warlords who were cunning enough to make a mega resource pile while society was on the way down, so they can get off-planet asap. Only once they're off planet do they realize the trap they fell into, when we cut off communications leaving them stranded on their spaceships, and we can truly begin the work of reshaping society.
